# Support

## Getting Help with TabCap

If you need assistance with TabCap, please check the information below or reach out to us.

## What is TabCap?

TabCap is a Safari extension that helps you limit the number of open tabs in your browser. It's designed to help you stay focused and avoid multitasking by automatically closing excess tabs when you exceed your set limit.

## Features

- **Tab Limit**: Set a maximum number of tabs (global or per window)
- **Allowlist**: Keep specific websites open even when the limit is reached
- **Statistics**: Track your tab usage over time
- **Easy Toggle**: Quickly enable or disable the extension

## Common Questions

### How do I install TabCap?

1. Download TabCap from the Mac App Store
2. Open Safari
3. Go to Safari > Settings > Extensions
4. Enable TabCap
5. Configure your tab limit in the extension popup

### How do I set my tab limit?

1. Click the TabCap icon in Safari's toolbar
2. Go to the Settings tab
3. Adjust the "Max Tabs" slider
4. Choose between "Global" (all windows) or "Per Window" limit

### How do I add websites to the allowlist?

1. Open the TabCap popup
2. Go to Settings
3. Enable "Allowlist"
4. Add URLs or domains that should always stay open

### The extension isn't working

- Make sure TabCap is enabled in Safari > Settings > Extensions
- Check that you've set a tab limit in the extension settings
- Try disabling and re-enabling the extension
- Restart Safari if needed

### Can I use TabCap on multiple Safari windows?

Yes! You can choose between:

- **Global Limit**: Total tabs across all Safari windows
- **Per Window Limit**: Maximum tabs per individual window

### Does TabCap collect my data?

No. TabCap stores all settings locally on your device and does not collect, transmit, or share any data. See our [Privacy Policy](PRIVACY_POLICY.md) for more details.
